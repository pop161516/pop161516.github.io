An analysis of

"How Frank Gehry and his team designed a monument to music"

- The story of The Walt Disney Concert Hall -

first impretions

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?
The colours popped out at me immediately! Using such a vibrant hue that covered most of the page was incredibly engaging. After the colour, I noticed some of the interaction, specifically the text following my mouse asking me to click for sound.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions?
I started by clicking to allow sound then I spent most of the two minutes playing with the mouse position, using the text brush and moving typography. When I eventually moved on, I spent the rest of the time watching the videos included at the beginning of chapter one. It was only after about 5 minutes that I got to the writing.

Goal of experience

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?
I think this page was created to educate people. It describes itself as a story and I think it does a good job by providing the reader with chapters and plenty of narrative along with image and video media. It showcases the history, process, and legacy of Frank Gehry.

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)
This site is accessible for reuse and rereading. Like any educational text, readers may only need to browse once but the content is laid out in chapters in case you need to find points or navigate. It has plenty of content that you may want to re-read and makes it entertaining to revisit. 

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?
The interactivity is focused at the beginning and end of the experience. This may indicate that the experience is designed to be replayed from start to end.  The interactivity begins less functionally and more aesthetically with things like mouse tracking and scroll-based navigation. But during the middle (making up a majority of the journey) there is little interaction besides parallax scrolling and video clips. The designers may have minimalised the interaction to encourage views to focus on content rather than style during the reading portion.

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?
The parallax scrolling and stretchy text were an interactive way of encouraging users to continue scrolling. It also maintains a similar style to a pop-up or interactive children’s book. This reinforces the aesthetic of a story and the goal of education. In the chapter portions of the site, the typography and its style in terms of colour and layout are similar to a news article. Very legible text with space for readability and images. Interactivity is used to emphasise creativity and engage readers in a story that might otherwise be long or un-interesting.

Pro’s and Con’s of design

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?
Minor details like the music looping or cutting out between chapters broke the immersion of the journey. The layout of the text and the use of padding and margins mean the type was heavily squished requiring readers to jump lines frequently and resulting in the site being excessively long. “Read more” boxes couldn’t be shrunk again making the page more difficult to navigate.

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?
The beginning page with the interactive text and drawings was fun and engaging. Very re-playable and creative. The parallax chapters broke up the story well and made the site easier to digest.
